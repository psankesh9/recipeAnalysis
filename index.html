Missingness Analysis

P-value for dependency of missingness in 'rating' on 'n_steps': 1.0

This result from the missingness analysis indicates that the missingness of ratings is not dependent on the number of steps in a recipe. In other words, it is unlikely that the complexity of a recipe influences whether or not it receives a rating, suggesting that the missing ratings are not NMAR (Not Missing At Random) with respect to the number of steps.

Hypothesis Testing

T-statistic: 1.042663633427837, P-value: 0.2971051945778797

The hypothesis testing aimed to determine whether the number of ingredients in a recipe significantly affects its average rating. The resulting p-value, which is above the conventional threshold of 0.05, leads us to fail to reject the null hypothesis. This indicates that there is no significant evidence to suggest that the number of ingredients has a substantial impact on the average rating of a recipe.

These findings collectively suggest that the complexity of a recipe, whether measured by the number of steps or ingredients, does not significantly influence its likelihood of being rated or its average rating. The insights gleaned from this analysis could be valuable for recipe creators and culinary enthusiasts, emphasizing other factors over complexity in recipe development and evaluation.
